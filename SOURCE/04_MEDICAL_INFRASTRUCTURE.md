## The Genetic Impossibility

### The SCN5A Overlap Problem

Long QT Syndrome Type 3 and Brugada Syndrome are both caused by variants in the **SCN5A gene**. Some variants, most notably **E1784K**, can cause both conditions simultaneously (the "overlap syndrome").

The McNamee family genetics:

| Family Member | Diagnosis | Brugada? | Genetic Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mother** | Long QT Syndrome, fitted with pacemaker/defibrillator | **NO** | Carries the family SCN5A variant; expresses LQTS but NOT Brugada |
| **Nicola** | Long QT Syndrome AND Brugada Syndrome (via Belfast Ajmaline) | **YES** | Supposedly expresses BOTH conditions from the same variant |
| **Brother** | Inherited cardiac condition (cascade screening) | Unknown | Cascade from mother's LQTS diagnosis |
| **Sister** | Inherited cardiac condition (cascade screening) | Unknown | Cascade from mother's LQTS diagnosis |

### The Contradiction

If the McNamee family carries an overlap variant causing both LQTS3 and Brugada, the mother should also show Brugada susceptibility. She does not. There are three possible explanations:

**(a) Variable penetrance.** Biologically possible, but requires an extraordinary coincidence: a Caucasian Irish woman outside every epidemiological risk factor for Brugada (91.3% male predominance, predominantly Southeast Asian populations) would be the one family member whose variant uniquely expresses Brugada, while the mother who carries the same variant does not.

**(b) Non-overlap variant.** The family SCN5A variant causes LQTS3 only. Nicola's Brugada would require a separate de novo mutation, exceptionally rare and immediately apparent on genetic testing.

**(c) The Brugada diagnosis is fabricated.** The family variant causes LQTS3, which is genuine. The Brugada diagnosis was added at the Belfast ICC via Ajmaline provocation. It cannot be verified because: the Ajmaline traces were never transferred, no genetic testing was ever performed, and comparative genetic sequencing of Nicola and her mother was never ordered.

### Why Genetic Testing Was Never Performed

This is the single most revealing absence. Nicola presents a textbook case: mother with LQTS and defibrillator, three affected siblings, dual diagnosis of LQTS3 and Brugada, SCN5A variant family. Every published guideline recommends genetic testing in this scenario.

Genetic testing was never performed. Never discussed. Never recommended. Never mentioned in any clinical note from any institution.

**This is the one test they could not fake.** A genetic test would either confirm the overlap variant or prove the Brugada diagnosis has no genetic basis. Either result would be dispositive. That is exactly why it was never ordered.
