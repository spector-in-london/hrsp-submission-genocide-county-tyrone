## Scenario 1: On-Table Kill (Bupivacaine)

### The Agent

Bupivacaine is a long-acting amide local anesthetic and the most cardiotoxic agent in its class. It blocks cardiac sodium channels with extremely slow recovery during diastole, unlike lidocaine which releases rapidly. This slow unbinding means that once bupivacaine reaches toxic cardiac concentrations, the sodium channel blockade is sustained and self-reinforcing.

### Mechanism of Death

1. Bupivacaine is administered during tumescent infiltration for liposuction, local anesthetic blocks for abdominoplasty, or wound infiltration. The planned procedures (mastopexy, abdominoplasty, liposuction, fat injection) require large volumes of local anesthetic, creating a substantial systemic absorption reservoir.

2. Systemic bupivacaine concentrations reach toxic levels. Unlike lidocaine toxicity, which typically presents with CNS symptoms (seizures, confusion) before cardiac toxicity, bupivacaine can produce direct cardiac toxicity without preceding neurological warning signs.

3. Bupivacaine toxicity produces ECG changes including ST elevation, widened QRS, Brugada-like coved ST pattern, and increased transmural dispersion of repolarization. Published research in porcine models confirms that "bupivacaine toxicity increases transmural dispersion of repolarization, developing of a Brugada-like pattern and ventricular arrhythmias" (Cardiovascular Toxicology, 2019). A case report in the Journal of the American College of Cardiology documents bupivacaine-induced cardiac toxicity "mimicking an acute non-ST segment elevation myocardial infarction."

4. The patient develops ventricular fibrillation progressing to asystole.

5. Standard Advanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS) resuscitation fails. An FDA review found that approximately 50% of bupivacaine-related cardiac accidents resulted in death despite full cardiopulmonary resuscitation. The only effective rescue is intravenous lipid emulsion therapy (intralipid), which may not be immediately available in every surgical facility.

### Post-Mortem Attribution

- ECG monitor may show ST elevation: **consistent with fabricated ischemic ECGs**

- Autopsy reveals structurally normal heart: **consistent with channelopathy and non-Q-wave MI**

- Medical record shows LQTS, Brugada, progressive ischemic disease: **cause of death is "obvious"**
