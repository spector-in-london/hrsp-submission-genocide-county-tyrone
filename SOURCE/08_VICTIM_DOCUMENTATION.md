## METHODOLOGY

This document presents victim profiles for each individual directly affected by the conduct described in this submission. The profiles follow HRSP's victim-centered documentation standard, adapted because the complainant witnesses are alive and available, a departure from most HRSP casework, where victims are typically identified post-mortem or post-displacement.

The adaptation is significant for HRSP: these are witnesses who can testify, submit to medical examination, produce documentary evidence, and cooperate with investigation if their safety can be secured. That is an investigative advantage HRSP rarely enjoys.

Terminology note: All individuals are designated "complainant witness" throughout this submission. This reflects their dual status as both victims of the conduct and witnesses to the institutional mechanisms through which it operated.

### Protected Characteristic

Nicola McNamee is an Irish Catholic woman from Greencastle, County Tyrone, a community defined by national, ethnic, and religious identity. Greencastle sits directly above the Curraghinalt gold deposit, the largest known gold deposit in the British Isles. Her community is the geographic center of organized resistance to the Dalradian mine, the most objected-to planning application in Northern Ireland history. Her family has lived in County Tyrone for generations.

### Circumstances of Targeting

**The Cascade Diagnosis:**

Nicola's mother was diagnosed first with Long QT Syndrome by the Belfast cardiac unit (NHS Inherited Cardiac Conditions Service) and fitted with a permanent pacemaker. Under the NHS mandatory cascade family screening protocol, Nicola was then compelled to attend Belfast ICC for testing. There, she was diagnosed with **two separate congenital heart defects**: Long QT Syndrome and Brugada Syndrome. These are genetically distinct channelopathies, they affect different cardiac ion channels (potassium channels in most Long QT variants; sodium channels in Brugada). Their co-occurrence in a single patient is exceptionally rare in the medical literature. Her half-brother and half-sister, toddlers at the time, were also compelled through cascade screening, diagnosed, and administered beta-blockers.

**Epidemiological Significance:**

The Brugada diagnosis is forensically extraordinary on its own. Peer-reviewed data establishes:

- 91.3% of 678 Brugada patients with arrhythmic events were male (Milman et al., *Heart Rhythm* 2018, DOI: 10.1016/j.hrthm.2018.06.019)

- Among Asian patients, male predominance reaches 98.1% (Milman et al., *Heart Rhythm* 2019, DOI: 10.1016/j.hrthm.2019.07.003)

- In a Western US hospital population, Brugada ECG pattern prevalence was only 0.14% (Donohue et al., *Am Heart Hosp J* 2008, DOI: 10.1111/j.1751-7168.2008.06418.x)

Nicola is a Caucasian Irish woman. She falls outside virtually every demographic risk factor for Brugada Syndrome. A dual diagnosis of Long QT *and* Brugada in a patient matching none of the epidemiological profile is statistically extraordinary.

She was told she had the "worst case in the family."

**The 2013 London Referral:**

Nicola was referred to a major London teaching hospital. The treating cardiologist, Jonathan Behar, stated that an "abnormally high percentage" of patients referred from County Tyrone were being diagnosed with Long QT and Brugada Syndrome. This is a clinician within the system raising the alarm, thirteen years ago. The statement has never been investigated.

**The 2023 KIMS Hospital Encounter:**

In November 2023, Nicola was seen at KIMS Hospital (Maidstone, Kent) for pre-surgical cardiac assessment. A cardiologist (Dr. Shaumik Adhya) noted the condition was "very faint" and indicated follow-up appointments were unnecessary. The encounter generated medical records that became the subject of forensic analysis (see Medical Infrastructure, Document 04).

**The Independent Refutation (January 2024):**

In January 2024, a cardiologist outside the UK examined Nicola and declared the cardiac diagnosis flatly wrong. Her ECG showed no cardiac abnormalities. She subsequently underwent general anesthesia, which is contraindicated for Long QT Syndrome, without any cardiac incident. If the diagnosis had been real, general anesthesia carried a significant risk of triggering a lethal cardiac event. Nothing happened.

**The diagnosis that shaped over a decade of Nicola's life, that propagated through her entire family, and that resulted in her mother receiving a permanent surgical implant, was wrong.**

### Harm Suffered

**If the originating diagnosis (Mrs. McNamee's) is false, and a non-UK cardiologist declared Nicola's cascade diagnosis flatly wrong in January 2024, indicating the entire diagnostic chain is compromised, then the pacemaker is an unnecessary invasive surgical intervention performed on the basis of a fabricated medical condition.**

A pacemaker implantation is not a diagnostic test, it is permanent surgical modification of the cardiac system. Mrs. McNamee now carries a device in her chest that she may not need, requiring regular clinical follow-up, device checks, and eventual generator replacement surgery.

Under 18 U.S.C. § 2340 (Torture), the intentional infliction of severe physical suffering through unnecessary medical procedures constitutes torture when committed under color of law or with state involvement. If the originating diagnosis was fabricated through an institutional mechanism operating under state intelligence architecture, the pacemaker implantation satisfies this threshold.
