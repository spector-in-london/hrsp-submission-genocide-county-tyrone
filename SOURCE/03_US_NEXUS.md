### CLOUD Act Dual Nationality Violation

Brian Spector holds dual US/UK citizenship. This creates a separate legal violation that compounds the jurisdictional basis.

The US-UK CLOUD Act bilateral data access agreement (effective October 2022) permits UK authorities to serve data orders directly on US communications providers for real-time interception and stored data of non-US persons, without notifying any US court or agency. The agreement contains a critical safeguard: if the target is a US person, the UK must notify the US and obtain US judicial authorization.

No US government body independently reviews UK CLOUD Act orders. No one verifies the UK's nationality classification of the target. The UK had direct incentive to classify Brian Spector as non-US in warrant applications: disclosing his US citizenship would trigger the agreement's mandatory US judicial oversight, oversight that would expose the surveillance to American authorities.

- Every surveillance action against him on American soil violated the agreement's US person protections

- The UK monitored a US citizen's communications with US federal law enforcement (FBI, congressional offices, this submission) without any American court or agency knowing

- The bilateral agreement was breached by deliberate misrepresentation

- The CLOUD Act's central safeguard failed because no US authority verifies UK nationality classifications

Nicola McNamee, as a UK citizen, has no CLOUD Act protections at all. The UK can surveil her communications on American soil through American companies with zero US oversight.
