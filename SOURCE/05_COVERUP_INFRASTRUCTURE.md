# 05, INSTITUTIONAL ANALYSIS: COVER-UP INFRASTRUCTURE

## Overview

The medical targeting documented in Part 1 (04) would be detectable in any normally functioning public health system. It is not detectable in County Tyrone because five separate institutional mechanisms, each independently documented, combine to create conditions where systematic falsification of cardiac diagnoses is structurally invisible. These mechanisms are not hypothetical. They are confirmed in published studies, government data, and public reporting.

---

## 1. Autopsy System Collapse

Northern Ireland has the **lowest hospital autopsy rate in the United Kingdom**: 0.46% (PubMed 2015).

| Period | NI Hospital Autopsy Rate |
|---|---|
| 1960s (UK-wide) | 40%+ |
| 1987 (NI) | 12% overall; 23% in teaching hospitals |
| 2013 (NI) | **0.46%** |

For comparison, Scotland's rate is 2.13%, more than four times higher.

Without autopsy, cause of death relies entirely on **clinical certification**, the treating doctor's written determination. In a population falsely diagnosed with inherited cardiac conditions that cause sudden death during sleep, clinical certification will record the cause as the pre-existing cardiac condition. No autopsy means no independent verification. No independent verification means no mechanism to detect that the diagnosis was fabricated.

**HRSP Significance:** A 0.46% autopsy rate is not a natural feature of a functioning health system. It is a structural condition that renders extrajudicial killings, disguised as cardiac deaths in a population pre-labelled with cardiac conditions, functionally undetectable.

## 2. 2015 Boundary Restructuring: County Tyrone Split into Thirds

In April 2015, Northern Ireland restructured from 26 to 11 local government councils. County Tyrone, the geographic unit where the suspected targeting is concentrated, was **split between three separate administrative districts**:

| New District | Tyrone Area Covered |
|---|---|
| **Mid Ulster** | Eastern Tyrone |
| **Fermanagh & Omagh** | Western Tyrone |
| **Derry City & Strabane** | Northern Tyrone (Strabane area) |

Each district uses **different data classification systems**. The *Irish News* reported in December 2025 that "lack of standardized data from councils thwarts any assessment of local government reform benefits." The Department for Communities concluded "it was too early in the process to conclude if local government reform has been cost effective or not due to the lack of supporting data."

**HRSP Significance:** County Tyrone no longer exists as a reportable statistical unit. Cardiac death data for the affected population is now fragmented across three districts, each using different classification standards. Any geographic clustering of anomalous diagnoses or deaths that would have been visible in county-level data is now invisible. This is not speculation, it is the documented consequence of the restructuring. Whether the restructuring was designed for this purpose or merely exploited for it, the effect is identical: statistical discontinuity that masks the pattern.

## 3. Tyrone County Hospital Closure (2017)

Tyrone County Hospital in Omagh, the only full-service hospital in County Tyrone, was **closed in 2017**. No replacement was ever built for its full services (maternity, A&E). This was the hospital that treated victims of the 1998 Omagh bombing.

The closure eliminates:

- **Local medical records**, institutional memory of diagnoses, treatments, and deaths in the County Tyrone population

- **Local clinical oversight**, the ability of local clinicians to observe patterns in their own patient population

- **Local forensic capacity**, any remaining autopsy or post-mortem capability in the geographic area

- **Patient access**, County Tyrone residents must now travel to Belfast, Enniskillen, or Derry for hospital services, increasing distance from their medical records and reducing continuity of care

**HRSP Significance:** The closure of the only hospital serving the affected population, with no replacement, removes the last institution that might have independently detected anomalous patterns in cardiac diagnoses or deaths within County Tyrone.

## 5. Molecular Autopsy Gap

A standard autopsy examines the body. A **molecular autopsy** goes further, it tests the deceased person's DNA to determine whether they actually carried a genetic cardiac condition. It is the only way to confirm or rule out an inherited heart condition after someone has died.

How often is this test performed in Northern Ireland? **We don't know.** The molecular autopsy referral rate for NI is not publicly available, it sits behind the same FOI firewall described in Section 4 (see Forensic Indicators Report, Indicator 5). What we do know is that across the entire 18-year period covered by the Kendall et al. study (2005–2023), only **five sudden cardiac deaths in children** were discovered through molecular autopsy, and all five were completely unknown to the Belfast ICC clinic. Five children in eighteen years, in a population of 478,416 under-19s.

The consequence of this gap is straightforward: if a person has been falsely diagnosed with Long QT Syndrome or Brugada and then dies suddenly, the doctor signing the death certificate will write "Long QT Syndrome" or "Brugada" as the cause. Without a molecular autopsy, no one will ever check whether that diagnosis was real. The false diagnosis follows the patient into the grave, and becomes the official cause of death.

### The Reverse Cascade: Diagnosing the Dead from the Living

There is a second mechanism that compounds the problem. When a young person dies suddenly and the autopsy is inconclusive, no obvious structural cause of death, standard clinical protocol calls for **screening the surviving family members** for inherited cardiac conditions. If a channelopathy is found in any relative, the dead person's cause of death is retrospectively attributed to that condition.

A 2013 study by Papadakis et al. (*Circulation: Arrhythmia and Electrophysiology*) examined 340 families of sudden cardiac death victims and found that in **51% of cases** where the autopsy findings were uncertain, the cause of death was determined by what was found in the living relatives, not by testing the deceased person's own DNA.

In a legitimate clinical context, this makes sense: if a family carries a genuine genetic mutation, it is reasonable to infer that the deceased may have carried it too. But in a context where the living family members are being falsely diagnosed, as documented in Part 1 (04), the mechanism works in reverse. The false diagnoses of the living become the official cause of death for the deceased. The fabrication is circular: the dead validate the diagnoses of the living, and the living validate the cause of death for the dead.

**HRSP Significance:** The molecular autopsy gap is the final seal on the system. A false cardiac diagnosis cannot be disproven after the patient dies, because the one test that could disprove it is almost never performed. And in cases where the autopsy is inconclusive, the cause of death is assigned not by testing the deceased, but by screening the surviving family, the same family that may already be carrying false diagnoses from the Belfast ICC cascade screening pipeline.

## The Combined Effect: Detection-Proof by Design

No single mechanism would be sufficient to conceal a two-decade campaign of fabricated cardiac diagnoses. But the five mechanisms operating simultaneously create a system in which detection is structurally impossible through normal public health channels:

| Mechanism | What It Eliminates |
|---|---|
| Autopsy collapse (0.46%) | Independent cause-of-death verification |
| Boundary restructuring (2015) | Geographic pattern visibility |
| Hospital closure (2017) | Local clinical oversight and institutional memory |
| FOI firewall | Public access to operational data |
| Molecular autopsy gap | Posthumous disproving of false diagnoses |

Each mechanism has an innocent explanation. Autopsy rates have declined across the UK. Boundary restructuring was a governance reform. Hospital closures happen for budgetary reasons. FOI processes are standard. Molecular autopsy is expensive.

But the combined effect creates conditions that are functionally identical to a purpose-built concealment architecture.

Whether these mechanisms were designed to conceal the targeting or merely exploited to that effect is a question for investigation. The effect is the same: a population can be falsely diagnosed, medicated, surgically implanted, and, potentially, killed, with no institutional mechanism capable of detecting the pattern.
